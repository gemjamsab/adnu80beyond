ADNU @ 80 & BEYOND

"Primum Regnum Dei – first, the Kingdom of God"


The Ateneo de Naga University began with Bishop Pedro P. Santos of Caceres. Having the need to strengthen the presence of Catholicism in the region, Bishop Santos envisioned the establishment of more schools within the diocese. During his time, there were only three Catholic schools in Bicol.

Thus, in 1939, he sent a message to the Society of Jesus through Fr. John Hurley, SJ, Superior of the Philippine Missions. The response from the Jesuit was favorable, as the spirit of Bishop Santos’s request aligned well with the mission of the Society of Jesus. After a series of visits and consultations, the first rector – Fr. Francis Burns, SJ – arrived in Naga City in 1940 to administer the Ateneo de Naga’s first iteration through the Camarines Sur Catholic Academy. More Jesuits were sent to Naga City to help with the management of the school.

Construction of Ateneo de Naga’s building in Bagumbayan Sur began in May 1941. On 8 December 1941, Fr Burns formally announced its completion.

Ateneo de Naga’s history has been an intricate cycle of challenges and victories. The school has witnessed itself on the brink of ends and closures, only to fight its way back to resurrection and, at times, reinvention. It survived World War II and Japanese fortification in the forties, threats of closure in the nineties, and the great global pandemic in 2020. Through the years the university, due to its geographical location has experienced numerous storms. Its structure and community have been tested, time and again – with the school and the community coming back stronger.

From a small Catholic school in the 1940s, to a big (and still growing) university in the 2020s — complete with academic services from basic education to higher education, along with offices which have forged partnerships with government and private institutions, the Ateneo de Naga University continues to march forward, for the greater glory of God!


ADNU is still the ONLY Autonomous and Institutionally Accredited University in Bicol!

The Commission on Higher Education under Memorandum Number 07, Series of 2024, granted the Ateneo de Naga University another three years of AUTONOMOUS STATUS until September 2027.

The Man Who Sent the Jesuits to Naga

by JOHN F. HURLEY, S.J.


The story of the 25th Anniversary of the founding of Bikol's Ateneo has a magnificent lesson and axample for all ATENEANS.

Twenty-five years ago your good Archbishop, the Most Reverend Pedro Santos, D.D., conceived the idea of a Catholic High School and College in Naga. From this cultural center there would radiate to every corner of Bicolandia, like rays of the sun, the light of Truth, Justice and Beauty.

The idea was electrifying. His Excellency was enthusiastic. He laid his plans carefully. Suddenly from unexpected quarters – Obstacles! Difficult Roadblocks!

What was the Archibishop to do? Was he to drop his grand plan for the greater honor and glory of God and for the benefit of Bicolandia? Not so. All his life he had great devotion to El Sagrado Corazon de Jesus. In Prayer he sought light and direction.

After some sleepless nights he saw clearly that these obstacles, these difficulties, these roadblocks against the great honor and glory of God and the good of Bicolandia were a sure sign that the "digitus Dei" - "the Finger of God" - was here.

Now as he looks back across a quarter of a century of self-sacrifice and dedication of the Fathers and Brothers and the Lay-Faculty, and the co-operation of the parents, students, alumni, alumnae, of the Civil Government Officials and friends of the Ateneo de Naga, the Archbishop ses that truly the "digitus Dei" - "the Finger of God" - was pointing to keep him firm and steady in his high resolve.

"REGNUM DEI" (if my memory serves me right) is the motto of your Ateneo de Naga - "Seek ye first the Kingdom of God and His Justice, and all thigns shall be added unto you." (Matt. 6,33).

The lesson of the story of the founding of the Ateneo is clear and simple. Your Motto "REGNUM DEI" exhorts you to seek first the kongdom of God and his Justice. Against any high resolve of yours the Devil will set dificulties, obstacles, roadblokcs, just as he did against Archbishop Santos twenty-five years ago. Then go back in memory a quarter of a century and, as did your Archbishop, pray to El Sagrado Corazon de Jesus and see that difficulties against a good work usually indicate the "Digitus Dei." May God belss us all!

Highlights and Dates

    1 June 1947 - Granted by the Department of Education full recognition as a standard four-year high school.

    5 June 1947 - College was opened with 87 male students.

    26 October 1953 - Became co-educational (college) with the admission of five women.

    1979 - Ateneo de Naga College and High School were the first in Camarines Sur to be accredited by PAASCU.

    1991 - the Department of Education, Culture, and Sports placed Ateneo de Naga in its list of excellent universities and colleges in the Philippines.

    11 November 1998 - University Status was granted by the Commission on Higher Education.

    14 May 2004 - ADNU established itself as the premier academic institution in the region by being the only school in Bicol with a PAASCU level III accredited status

    23 June 2003 - ADNU High School community moved to Pacol, and became co-education with the admission of 185 girls.

    2 June 2008 - ADNU became the only university in Southern Luzon to be granted by CHED with Autonomous Status.

    2013 - ADNU expanded its basic education community by opening Grade School campus in Pacol.

    2013 - Piloted Senior High School (First semester began in 2015).

    13 June 2017 - ADNU opened the College of Law.

    2024 - ADNU retains its Autonomous Status.
